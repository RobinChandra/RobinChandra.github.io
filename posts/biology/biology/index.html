Molecular Biology - Isoelectric Point of Proteins

Q1

The following sequence of template DNA encodes a protein called Pz.Kpfw.VI:

5’-GGTGTCTACATACCACATGGTAGTCTTGGTGCCCCATGCGTCAGAGTGAATCGCCCGTAAATATTTGGTGTGTACCAGCTAACAACTGTACAGGTGT-3’

Q1.A

Using the table in Appendix A, identify the polypeptide sequence synthesized! Write your answer from N- to -C and use 3 letters notation
(e.g N– GLY ALA VAL –C)

Q1.B

Determine the point of isoelectric of Pz.Kpfw.VI! Use Appendix B.

Q1.C

Trypsin (N-Lys/Arg-C*) is added to the solution containing Pz.KpfwVI. Determine how many fragments formed (if any) after incubation for 5 minutes at T=310K.

Q1.D

Determine the pI of each new fragment(s) (if any). Use Appendix B.

Q1.E

Determine the net charge of Pz.Kpfw.VI and all its fragments (if any) in a solution with pH=(2.34, 5.67, 8.97, 12.86)

Q1.F

Explain briefly why the incubation must be carried out at T=310K!

Q1.G

Calculate the pH of the solution and determine the charge to mass ratio (z/(g/mol)) of Pz.Kpfw.VI and all it fragments (if any) in a buffer solution with the following composition: [Na2HPO4] = 0.1M, [NaHPO4-] = 2.3x10-6 M, pKa Na2HPO4 = 12.36, amino acid molecular weight = 110 Da. Round all your answer to 3 decimals point.

Q1.H

Name the two shortest fragment from Q.1C as Pz.I and Pz.II. Another two polipeptide, Pz.III and Pz.IV with charge to mass ratio of - 8.613 x 10-5 and + 8.871 x 10-5 (z/Da) respectively were added to the gel. The fragments were injected in lanes located at the midplane (marked by [ x ]) of a vertically oriented polyacrylamide gel. Determine and draw the approximate location of each fragment’s band after running the gel in moderately high voltage for a while. The location of electrode is marked by [ + ] and [ - ]. Gravitational force pulls downwards.
